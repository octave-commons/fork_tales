# Gates of Truth

## Chapter 4 — After Logout

The band disengages with a soft internal click.

No chime.
No fanfare.

Just gravity.

莉津律宗利都 lies still for a moment, eyes open to the low ceiling of a Tokyo apartment that feels smaller every month. The air is colder than Lattice City. The silence heavier.

They flex their right hand first.

Then the left.

Then the knee.

There is no knife.

But memory moves like one.

They sit up carefully.

The room resolves the way the game does—but slower. Light bleeding in from the window. Dust visible in suspension. A train passing somewhere beyond concrete.

The trains never stopped.

They swing their legs over the side of the bed.

Weight.

This world negotiates less.

They stand.

A micro-hesitation. Not pain. Anticipation of pain.

Muscle remembering a different version of itself.

"It’s fine," they murmur aloud.

Voice feels stranger here than in the game.

They cross to the kitchenette, leaning slightly before realizing they are leaning.

Correcting.

Annoyed.

The kettle clicks on.

Steam begins its slow rise.

On the table, a tablet displays static system news in clean 日本語-English.

> GLOBAL INFRASTRUCTURE REMAINS STABLE
> CROSS-FORK SYNCHRONIZATION WITHIN SAFE PARAMETERS
> NO ANOMALOUS CASCADES DETECTED

They stare at the word safe.

Safe from what?

The screen brightness adjusts automatically.

Somewhere in Moscow, Null is probably still awake.
Somewhere in Shenzhen, Patch is optimizing something that already works.
Somewhere at Seishin Infrastructure, Sei is monitoring dependency graphs like rail lines.
Somewhere in America, Duct is not sleeping enough.

莉津律宗利都 lifts the cup.

Steam curls upward.

For a moment, it looks like the Gate’s hum made visible.

Ridiculous.

They take a sip.

Heat spreads.

Outside, a train glides past with mechanical indifference.

The spine holds.

For now.

莉津律宗利都 sets the cup down.

The band rests on the table beside the tablet.

Small.

Unassuming.

They look at it the way someone looks at a door that opens both ways.

"Just another day," they say.

And for the first time, the words feel heavier outside the game than inside it.
