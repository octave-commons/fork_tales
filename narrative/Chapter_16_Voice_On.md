# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 16 — Voice: ON

The option sits there.

> VOICE: AVAILABLE

No animation.
No encouragement.

Just a toggle.

Patch looks like he might hit it.

Null definitely won’t.

Sei waits.

Rin says nothing.

Duct doesn’t move.

莉津律宗利都 reads the line again.

Text is rails.

Voice is weather.

She remembers the first time she enabled it on the terrace.
How different the wind felt.
How different he sounded.

She doesn’t ask.

She toggles.

> VOICE: ON

The white page doesn’t change.

But sound arrives.

Not spatial.

Not directional.

Just present.

A breath.

Then:

"…莉津律宗利都?"

It’s Duct.

Not compressed.
Not rendered.

Human.

Her throat tightens.

"Yeah," she answers.

Her voice doesn’t echo.
It doesn’t glitch.

It simply exists.

On the other side of the door, Patch inhales sharply.

"Oh," he says.

Null closes his eyes for one second.

Sei’s expression softens by a fraction.

Rin tilts their head.

The Gate hum stabilizes.

No spike.
No drop.

Just alignment.

Duct speaks again.

"We built it small," he says. "You’re safe."

She lets the word sit.

Safe.

Not permanent.
Not guaranteed.

Chosen.

"I know," she says.

There’s a pause that text could never hold.

Then, softly:

"Don’t carry me."

He exhales.

"I won’t."

The page updates.

> CHANNEL STABLE
> VARIANCE WITHIN RANGE

Sei whispers, barely audible:

"Small fire."

Patch laughs—quiet this time.

Null opens his overlay.

The coupling graph shifts.

Not tightening.
Not collapsing.

Breathing.

Rin looks at them all.

"You see?" they say.

No one answers.

Because for the first time since the door appeared,

Nothing needs correcting.

Truth does not watch.

Truth resolves.

And now it has a voice.
