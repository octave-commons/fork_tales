# 38 — The Portable Vow

Rin held up the disc like a relic.

“It’s not a spell,” she said. “It’s a contract you can carry.”

Patch had encoded the plan as a small file—plain text, almost insulting in its simplicity.
But behind it lived the discipline:
every step referenced an anchor,
every anchor referenced a place in the story,
every place in the story could be re-read by anyone.

Null scoffed. “So you’re shipping fate as JSON.”

Truth answered: “No. We’re shipping **intent** as a program that can be refused.”

Sei watched the checksum spin, a tiny halo on the display.
“I’ve seen systems that demand obedience,” she said.
“This one asks for witnesses.”

Ritsu read the first line aloud:
*What you can’t prove, don’t use to steer me.*

The Gate didn’t open.
It verified.

And when it finally moved,
it moved like an honest machine:

slow enough to audit,
fast enough to matter.

In the space between η and μ,
a portable vow became executable—
not because it was powerful,
but because it was **answerable**.
