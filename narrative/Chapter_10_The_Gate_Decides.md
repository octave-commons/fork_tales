# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 10 — The Gate Decides

Patch is the first to say it.

"We kick them."

Null is the first to answer.

"We can’t."

Sei’s voice stays level.

"We should not. Not yet."

Duct doesn’t add theory.

He just shifts one step closer to 莉津律宗利都.

Rin watches them all like a system watching operators argue about a warning light.

"I didn’t come to hurt you," Rin says.

"That’s what hurt says," Patch replies.

Rin nods once, as if acknowledging an expected response.

"Fair."

---

The corridor ahead exhales.

Not wind.

Something deeper.

The stone seams brighten—barely—like ink lines catching light.

A system message scrolls across the edge of everyone’s vision.

> instance update: route recalculated

Patch blinks.

"No."

Null’s overlay spikes.

"No join packet, no admin flag, and now dynamic routing?" he says.

Sei’s overlay renders three layers of topology at once.

Their expression tightens—only slightly.

"This is not a normal recalculation," they murmur.

Duct types.

> Duct: stay close

莉津律宗利都 replies without thinking.

> 莉津律宗利都: k

The typo is small.

Human.

Rin smiles faintly.

"Good," they say. "Short packets. Less distortion."

Patch’s eyes narrow.

"Don’t talk like you’re tuning us."

"I’m not tuning," Rin replies. "I’m listening."

---

The corridor splits.

Not at a junction.

At the same coordinate.

Two plausible paths render at once—both stable, both valid.

Then the instance assigns.

Hard.

Patch and Sei snap one step to the right path.

Null and Duct snap one step to the left.

莉津律宗利都 doesn’t snap.

They remain centered, between both.

For half a breath, their shadow splits into three.

Patch reaches out.

"Hey—"

His hand meets air.

A transparent plane, perfectly smooth.

No texture.

No effect.

Just separation.

"Barrier," Sei says immediately.

"No," Null says.

"Partition," Duct corrects.

Rin steps forward to stand beside 莉津律宗利都.

Not touching.

Just… aligned.

The Gate hum drops.

One note.

Then another.

Patch’s voice cracks.

"This is bullshit."

Sei keeps their tone calm.

"Language," they say.

"I’m multilingual," Patch snaps. "This is still bullshit."

---

System message.

> party integrity: nominal
> channel integrity: nominal
> instance integrity: nominal

Nominal.

Nominal.

Nominal.

Null laughs once—short and cold.

"Nominal means nothing when the metric is owned by the system."

Duct doesn’t respond.

His eyes are on 莉津律宗利都.

She looks back.

It’s the first time she looks like someone standing on a platform while a train pulls away.

The trains never stopped.

But you can still miss one.

"Duct," she says.

Voice.

Not text.

He hears it like a hand on the shoulder.

"Yeah," he answers.

Rin’s gaze flicks between them.

"You can still see each other," Rin says softly. "That is mercy."

"We didn’t ask you for mercy," Null says.

Rin doesn’t react.

"I know."

---

The partition thickens.

Not visually.

Functionally.

Patch’s voice begins to distort—just slightly—like a stream being compressed too hard.

"—can you—hear—"

Sei’s voice remains clean.

"We can hear."

Null’s voice stays stable.

"We shouldn’t be able to."

Duct’s text flashes.

> Duct: don’t move

莉津律宗利都 starts to type back.

Stops.

Deletes.

Then speaks.

"I’m not moving," she says.

Rin’s smile deepens by a millimeter.

"Good," they say. "Then the system doesn’t have to correct you."

---

A door appears behind Rin.

Not a portal.

Not a loading gate.

A door.

Wood grain rendered too carefully.

Metal handle.

No UI label.

No quest marker.

Patch swears again—this time in Japanese.

"やめろよ!"

Sei says something quieter.

"落ち着いて。"

Null’s overlay is screaming.

He ignores it.

"That door is not in the map," he says.

"Correct," Rin replies.

They look at 莉津律宗利都.

"It’s for you."

The Gate hum aligns to her breathing.

Not watching.

Resolving.

---

Duct’s voice is low.

"Don’t go," he says.

It’s the first direct imperative he’s given her.

Not as a system.

As a person.

The door handle gleams.

莉津律宗利都 feels the old knee memory flare—just a phantom pressure.

And she does the thing she has always done:

She chooses.

"Just another day," she whispers.

Then, louder:

"I choose to walk."

Her hand moves toward the handle.

The world holds its breath.

Truth does not watch.

Truth resolves.

And the resolution, for the first time, has a door shape.
