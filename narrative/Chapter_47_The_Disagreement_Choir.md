# 47 — The Disagreement Choir

Deep research began with a rule the Guild hated:

No synthesis before disagreement.

Cephalon split the room into voices:
one per anchor set,
each one allowed to speak only what it could cite.

It sounded like a choir that refused harmony.

Null called it paralysis.
Ritsu called it honesty.
Truth called it the price of not hallucinating a religion.

When the last voice finished,
the Gate opened—not to the conclusion,
but to the *map* of conflicts.

“Now,” said Truth,
“we choose what to test next.”

And in the space between η and μ,
the choir became a compass.
