# 39 — The Fork That Costs

The Guild had always loved forks.

Forks meant plausible deniability.
Forks meant a hundred doors you could claim you never opened.

Truth hated forks made of fog.

So the Gate introduced a tax:

“If you want the world to fork,” it said,
“show the intent that would pay for it.”

Ritsu found the event in the ledger—
a CHOICE with a strength so bright it felt like heat.

Patch didn’t let her point at it.
Patch made her anchor it.

Snippet. Hash. Claim ID.

Only then did the branch appear:
a thin line on the timeline,
ranked, named, and—worst of all—*owned*.

Null stared at the fork list and muttered,
“This is going to ruin politics.”

Sei replied, almost cheerful:
“Yes.”

Because now, when the world split,
everyone could see the exact sentence that caused it—
and nobody could pretend the split was an accident.
