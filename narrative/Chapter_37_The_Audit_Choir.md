# 37 — The Audit Choir

They expected the Gate to feel like judgment.

Instead it felt like **singing in a room full of auditors**.

Every time someone tried to speak a claim too cleanly,
Truth would answer with a question that wasn’t a question:

“**Where is it anchored?**”

Ritsu learned to keep her hands open.
No clenched fists, no hidden cards.
Just the stone, the snippet, the hash.

Patch added a tiny feature to the overlay:
a chorus track that played whenever an anchor passed review.

It wasn’t a fanfare.
It was a single chord—soft, steady—like a heartbeat confirming:

*Yes. This is real enough to use.*

Null hated it on principle.
Then he caught himself listening for it.

Sei, who had lived her whole life inside systems that never admitted fault,
stood very still.

“This,” she said, “is the sound of power being forced to show its work.”

Rin nodded. “And the sound of story being forced to grow up.”

Duct leaned close to Ritsu, almost smiling.

“Welcome to the audit choir,” he whispered.
“Where truth isn’t a vibe—
it’s a harmony you can verify.”

Ritsu felt the space between η and μ tighten like a string pulled to pitch,
and she understood:

If you want reality to fork,
you don’t shout.

You **commit**.

And if you want people to follow you,
you don’t charm.

You **anchor**.
