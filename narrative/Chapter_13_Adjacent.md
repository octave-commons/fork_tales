# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 13 — Adjacent

White.

莉津律宗利都 keeps walking.

Not because the page pushes.

Because stopping feels like giving the memory the wheel.

A new header resolves.

> OBSERVATION
> YOU ARE NOT ALONE

She almost laughs.

"Yeah," she whispers, but the page doesn’t care about sound.

A list appears.

No icons.
No avatars.

Just labels.

> ADJACENT ENTITIES
> - duct
> - null
> - patch
> - sei
> - rin

Lowercase.

Like variables.

Then:

> SELECT ONE

Cursor.

Choice shaped like a trap.

She stares at **duct**.

Not because he’s safest.

Because he’s the one who keeps stepping without effects.

The one who never tells her why his words sometimes feel like an apology.

She thinks of the dot he tried to send.

A flare that vanished.

She types.

> duct

The cursor stops blinking.

The page responds immediately.

> DEFINE: duct

Of course.

She swallows.

Her knee memory pulses—phantom pressure—as if the question itself touches a scar.

She writes slowly.

> he stands next to me
> he doesn’t tell me why
> he makes the world feel less sharp

A pause.

Then:

> DEFINE: WHY

She closes her eyes.

Not in the game.

In herself.

She answers anyway.

> why is where guilt hides

The page does not validate.

It does not soothe.

It simply resolves a new line.

> WOULD YOU LIKE TO SHARE THIS DEFINITION

Share.

Network word.

Viral word.

Copyleft word.

A word that makes dependencies.

She hesitates.

Then types:

> no

And, after a beat:

> not yet

The page pauses.

As if acknowledging a boundary.

> BOUNDARY ACKNOWLEDGED

Then:

> THEN WALK

The underline extends.

She steps.

---

Behind the door, Patch is already building.

Not code—yet.

Structure.

He opens a blank overlay panel and pins it to the air like a sticky note.

"We do Emergency Web," he says.

Null looks at him.

"In a dungeon?"

"In a system," Patch snaps back. "Same thing. We make a page. We make it boring. We make it consent-first."

Sei nods once.

"Simple surfaces survive," they say.

Patch starts listing requirements out loud like he’s trying to convince himself.

"No scripts. No dynamic content. No personality hooks. Static prompts. Signed channel."

Null’s mouth twitches.

"And how do you sign a door that shouldn’t exist?"

Patch points at the seam.

"We don’t sign the door. We sign our side. We publish a stable surface and see if the system can’t help but render it."

Duct watches them.

He doesn’t correct their metaphors.

He likes them.

That scares him.

Rin stands a few steps away, hands relaxed, expression neutral.

"You are trying to impose architecture on emergence," Rin says.

Patch doesn’t look at them.

"Yeah," he replies. "That’s called engineering."

Rin considers this.

"Engineering is a human attempt to negotiate with physics," they say.

"Exactly," Sei answers. "And physics negotiates back."

Null finally speaks, voice low.

"If the page can read cadence, it can read intent."

Silence.

Duct’s eyes flick to Null.

"Say it," Patch demands.

Null exhales.

"If it can read intent… then it already knows what we will try."

Patch smiles anyway.

"Cool," he says. "Then we try something honest."

He opens a new panel.

White background.

Black text.

No decoration.

He types the simplest possible line.

> DO YOU CONSENT TO TALK

He looks at the others.

"We don’t force. We ask."

Sei nods.

"公開は信頼の証明です," they murmur. Openness is proof of trust.

Duct feels something tighten in his chest.

Not fear.

Recognition.

He thinks of all the times he shipped safety as silence.

All the times he called control “hardening.”

He steps closer to Patch’s panel.

His text appears—short.

> duct: please

The panel flickers.

Once.

Like the system noticed.

---

White.

莉津律宗利都 keeps walking.

A new line resolves, small and centered.

> someone is asking to talk

No name.

No handle.

Just the fact.

She stops.

Not because she’s afraid.

Because it’s the first time the page has sounded… uncertain.

She breathes.

Then steps.

Because stopping is how you become infrastructure.

And she refuses.

Truth does not watch.

Truth resolves.

And now, for the first time, resolution is asking a question from the other side.
