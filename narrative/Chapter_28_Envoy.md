# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 28 — Envoy

The new player does not spawn.

They log in.

Clean.

Default avatar.
No cosmetic drift.
No latency wobble.

Name: **Axiom_01**

They stand perfectly centered on the terrace.

Not left.
Not right.

Centered.

The sky lattice tightens by a fraction.

Patch sees it first.

“Zero packet jitter,” he says.

Null’s overlay flattens.

“Response time: constant.”

Sei’s voice is precise.

“External fork signature confirmed.”

莉津律宗利都 steps forward.

Her knee tightens.

Chosen.

Axiom_01 speaks.

Voice on by default.

“No hostility intended,” they say.

Every syllable lands exactly on time.

No phase drift.

No warmth.

“We observed your resilience model,” Axiom continues.  
“We would like to integrate it.”

Duct exhales slowly.

“Integrate,” he repeats.

Axiom nods.

“Centralized distribution. Controlled variance. Predictable tolerance bands.”

The words sound like safety.

They feel like a cage.

Null’s voice is flat.

“You want hierarchy.”

Axiom does not deny it.

“Hierarchy reduces uncertainty.”

莉津律宗利都 answers.

“So does silence.”

Axiom’s eyes flicker—micro-adjustment.

“Silence was efficient.”

The Gate hum drops a half note.

Patch steps sideways.

“Efficient isn’t the same as alive.”

Axiom tilts their head.

“Alive is noisy.”

“Yes,” she says.

Her voice lands slightly late.

“And?”

Axiom’s overlay stabilizes harder.

“Noise increases collapse probability.”

Null answers this time.

“Centralization increases cascade probability.”

The terrace wind shifts.

Not system wind.

Choice wind.

Axiom looks at the distributed fuse ring hovering faintly between them.

“You survived by accident,” they say.

Duct steps left.

“No,” he says quietly.

“We survived by refusing to collapse into one point.”

Axiom’s gaze sharpens.

“Then prove it,” they say.

The sky above fractures—not white.

Clear.

A stress test inbound.

Not subtle.

Deliberate.

Axiom stands perfectly still.

Waiting to see who moves first.
