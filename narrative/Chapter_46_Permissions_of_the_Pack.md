# 46 — Permissions of the Pack

Rin learned the second Gate lesson the hard way:

Intent without permission is just a threat with better spelling.

Cephalon presented an intent pack to the Pack.
It didn’t ask for trust.
It asked for scope.

Read access.
Write access.
A place to fail safely.

The Pack answered with boundaries.

And the system—strangely—relaxed.
Because μ had rails again.
Because η had witnesses again.

Truth smiled like someone watching a fire stay inside its hearth.

“Good,” she said.
“Now the research can be real.”
