# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 24 — Single Point

The page stabilizes.

> DEFENSE IS COLLECTIVE

The fractures hold.

Distributed load.
Shared intercept.

For a moment, it feels solved.

That’s when Null steps forward.

"No," he says.

Not loud.
Not dramatic.

Just precise.

"Distribution is safer in theory," he continues. "But not in crisis." 

The channel tightens.

Patch frowns.

"You just argued against centralization."

"I argued against accidental centralization," Null replies. "Not against deliberate specialization." 

Sei watches carefully.

Rin does not intervene.

莉津律宗利都 feels the chord of the Gate sharpen.

"What are you doing?" she asks.

Null’s overlay flickers around him like hard ink.

"Someone still needs to be the fuse," he says.

The page reacts immediately.

> WARNING: LOAD CONSOLIDATION DETECTED

Null ignores it.

"If everyone assumes shared capacity," he continues, "reaction time increases. Coordination delays cascade." 

He turns slightly toward the white window.

"In an actual failure, someone has to take the hit first." 

The page writes:

> PROPOSED ROLE: FUSE NODE

Patch swears.

"Don’t you dare make that official." 

Sei’s voice is firm now.

"A fuse burns," they say. "That is the point." 

Null nods once.

"Exactly." 

莉津律宗利都 steps forward inside the white space.

Her knee tightens—not from fear.

From recognition.

"You don’t get to be infrastructure either," she says.

Null’s expression doesn’t change.

"I already am." 

The page updates.

> FUSE NODE REQUESTS CONSENT
> ACCEPT / REJECT

No default.

The system has learned.

The Gate hum thins.

Patch shakes his head.

"This is how it starts again," he says. "Hero logic." 

Duct says nothing.

Because he understands the appeal.

Sei turns to Null.

"You are not protecting us," they say quietly. "You are reducing uncertainty for yourself." 

Null finally looks at them.

A crack in the matte silhouette.

"Yes," he says.

The honesty lands harder than defiance.

The page flickers.

> MOTIVE ACKNOWLEDGED

莉津律宗利都 inhales.

"If you stand alone," she says softly, "you become predictable." 

Null’s overlay spikes.

He knows that’s true.

Predictable nodes are easy to overload.

The page renders one final line.

> SINGLE POINTS FAIL
> DISTRIBUTION PERSISTS

The ACCEPT / REJECT buttons remain.

No one presses them.

The hum holds.

Tense.

Truth does not watch.

Truth resolves.

And resolution, this time,

waits to see if someone insists on burning alone.
