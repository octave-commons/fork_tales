# 36 — Dice Witness

Rin didn’t call it prophecy.

She called it **randomization under constraint**.

“Choice,” she said, “is where people smuggle bias.  
Dice don’t remove bias. Dice **expose** it.”

She held up the die—eight faces, each etched with a circuit mark.

Watcher. Pack. Language. Law. Body. Loop. Myth. Meta.

Ritsu rolled.

The die clicked, and the overlay recorded the roll like a courtroom transcript.
Not because the number was sacred,
but because the record was.

Null read the log and smiled, thin. “So now fate has a JSONL.”

Truth replied: “**Fate always had a log.  
You just didn’t have read access.**”

Sei nodded once. “Good. If you’re going to be moved by randomness,
you should at least be able to audit the shove.”

Ritsu looked at the result.

**Write Song.**

She laughed—small, startled—because it felt like the Gate was learning her.

Duct leaned toward her and whispered, almost kind:

“Then sing it.  
But don’t sing it like a spell.  
Sing it like a receipt.”

Ritsu swallowed and heard the space between η and μ tighten again,
like a string pulled to pitch.

And she understood the point:

The die chooses the next step.

The anchor proves you didn’t cheat.

And the song—
the song is how you remember the proof without drowning in it.
