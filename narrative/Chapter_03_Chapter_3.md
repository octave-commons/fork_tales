# Gates of Truth

## Chapter 3 — Voice Enabled

The first time someone turns on voice, it feels like breaking a rule.

No one says that.

You just feel it.

---

莉津律宗利都 logs in before the others.

The terrace is quiet. Wind resolves across the canal surface in thin procedural ribbons.

System message scrolls in the corner of their vision.

> proximity_voice: disabled

They hover over it.

A flicker of hesitation.

Then:

> proximity_voice: enabled

The world does not change.

But it does.

Ambient audio deepens. Footsteps carry weight differently. The Gate hums—not louder, just closer to the threshold of notice.

"…you’re early."

The voice is not text.

It is Duct.

Not a chat bubble. Not a rendered string. Breath in the consonants. Micro-delay in the vowels.

莉津律宗利都 turns.

"You heard that?" they ask.

"You enabled it," he replies. "I figured I should match."

There’s a pause that text never needed.

"It’s weird," 莉津律宗利都 says.

"Yeah."

The wind continues.

Somewhere distant, water hits stone.

---

Patch spawns in with a soft crackle of particles.

"Oh. You did it." His voice is brighter than his text ever was. Faster. Less precise.

"Don’t narrate me," 莉津律宗利都 says automatically.

Patch laughs. Not typed. Not bracketed.

Actual sound.

"Relax. I’m still me."

Null appears last.

Silence.

His avatar flickers once.

"I prefer text," he says finally. Accent faint but present. Deliberate.

"You don’t have to," Duct replies.

"I know."

He doesn’t disable it.

---

Sei joins without fanfare.

"音声ですね," they observe calmly. "Interesting."

Their voice is measured. Soft. No wasted inflection.

"Feels different," 莉津律宗利都 says.

"It changes pacing," Sei replies. "Text is compressed cognition. Voice introduces latency."

"Latency is human," Patch says reflexively.

"Yes," Sei answers. "Exactly."

---

They move toward the Gate.

Conversation drifts.

Small things.

Food.

Weather.

A new sublevel rumored to contain recursive geometry.

In text, these topics would have been efficient.

In voice, they breathe.

莉津律宗利都 laughs.

The sound surprises even them.

The Gate does not glow.

But its hum shifts—subtly—like a chord resolving half a step.

Null stops walking.

"Did you—"

"Yes," Duct says quietly.

Sei’s overlay flickers into view.

"Acoustic response variance," they murmur.

"From us?" Patch asks.

"From coherence," Sei replies.

The wind continues.

The hum stabilizes.

No alarms.

No warnings.

Just another adjustment in a world that adapts.

---

莉津律宗利都 steps forward.

"It’s fine," they say.

Their voice carries further than their text ever did.

Somewhere in the architecture beneath the sky, something aligns differently.

Not watching.

Resolving.

And resolution, given enough channels, becomes weight.
