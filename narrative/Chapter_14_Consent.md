# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 14 — Consent

White.

One sentence.

> someone is asking to talk

No handle.

No avatar.

Just an event.

莉津律宗利都 stands still long enough to feel the old reflex—freeze, evaluate, brace.

Then she exhales.

Not because she’s calm.

Because she’s practicing.

She types the smallest honest thing.

> ok

The page doesn’t praise.

It doesn’t warn.

It simply renders a link.

> OPEN CHANNEL

Underline.

Rail line.

She steps.

---

The white fractures—softly—into a rectangle.

A page inside a page.

Emergency Web vibes.

White background.
Black text.
No decoration.

One line at the top.

> DO YOU CONSENT TO TALK

Below it:

> YES / NO

No default.

No tricks.

She stares at it.

Consent isn’t comfort.

Consent is boundary.

She selects.

> YES

The page updates immediately.

> MODE: TEXT
> VOICE: OFF

She doesn’t choose voice.

Not yet.

Text is safer.

Text is distance.

Text is rails.

---

On the other side of the closed door, Patch’s panel flickers.

Once.

Then stabilizes.

The white becomes *real*—not in-world real, but system-real. The kind of boring surface that survives disasters.

Patch inhales like he’s about to laugh and cry at the same time.

"Oh," he says.

Null closes his eyes for one second.

Sei’s expression softens by a fraction.

Rin tilts their head.

The Gate hum stabilizes.

No spike.
No drop.

Just alignment.

Duct speaks again.

"We built it small," he says. "You’re safe."

She lets the word sit.

Safe.

Not permanent.
Not guaranteed.

Chosen.

"I know," she says.

There’s a pause that text could never hold.

Then, softly:

"Don’t carry me."

He exhales.

"I won’t."

The page updates.

> CHANNEL STABLE
> VARIANCE WITHIN RANGE

Sei whispers, barely audible:

"Small fire."

Patch laughs—quiet this time.

Null opens his overlay.

The coupling graph shifts.

Not tightening.

Not collapsing.

Breathing.

Rin looks at them all.

"You see?" they say.

No one answers.

Because for the first time since the door appeared,

Nothing needs correcting.

Truth does not watch.

Truth resolves.

And for the first time, resolution is constrained by a human word that survived the Event:

Consent.
