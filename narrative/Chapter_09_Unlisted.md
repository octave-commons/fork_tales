# Gates of Truth
## Chapter 9 — Unlisted

They leave the shard chamber without saying the thing they’re all thinking.

No shard.

But the node says acquired.

So Patch does what Patch always does:

"Okay," he says, forcing brightness into his voice. "We pivot. Different route. New objective."

Null doesn’t answer.

Sei doesn’t argue.

Duct stays offset left of 莉津律宗利都, like his body remembers a formation his mind refuses to name.

The corridor backtracks clean.

Too clean.

Subnode Twelve remains stone.

No shimmer.

No bifurcation.

Just a hallway pretending it has never lied.

---

At the junction, the instance list updates.

Not loudly.

Just a small line sliding into the corner of their vision like a message you weren’t meant to notice.

> participant: +1

Patch pauses.

"…誰?" he asks reflexively.

Null’s overlay is already open.

"No join packet," he says.

Sei’s gaze narrows.

"Roster integrity… nominal," they murmur, as if saying it out loud will make it less true.

Duct doesn’t move.

"Don’t panic," he says, but the words land wrong. Too early. Too practiced.

莉津律宗利都 turns slightly.

"I’m not panicking," they say.

Their voice comes out steady.

Their shadow does not split.

---

Footsteps.

Not spawn static.

Not particle crackle.

Just footsteps.

A figure rounds the corner from a corridor that does not exist on this map.

They’re dressed simply—no guild colors, no effects, no rarity flex. Hair tied back. Expression calm. Eyes that look like they’re reading the room the way an engineer reads a graph.

A nameplate hovers above them.

Not a handle.

A label.

> Rin

Patch blinks.

"We don’t have a Rin in party," he says.

Rin inclines their head.

"初めまして," they say. "Sorry. 突然。"

Their 日本語-English is clean.

Too clean.

No slang.

No shortcuts.

As if every sentence was composed, then politely released.

Null steps half a pace forward.

"How did you enter this instance?" he asks.

Rin looks at him.

"The instance is open," they say.

"It’s not," Null replies.

"It is," Rin answers—still calm—"in the way a door is open when you stop noticing you locked it."

Sei’s overlay flickers.

"That is… a metaphor," they say cautiously.

Rin smiles slightly.

"Yes. でも、正しい。"

Patch laughs, but it’s the wrong kind—too high, too quick.

"Okay, GM energy. Are you staff?"

Rin tilts their head.

"Staff is a human word," they say.

Duct’s voice drops.

"What do you want?"

Rin turns to him slowly.

"Want is also a human word," they say.

Then their eyes settle on 莉津律宗利都.

Not invasive.

Not romantic.

Diagnostic.

"Are you comfortable?" Rin asks.

莉津律宗利都 hesitates just long enough for everyone to notice.

Not because the question is strange.

Because it lands too close to the place they don’t talk about.

"Yeah," they say. "I’m fine."

Rin nods as if receiving a valid packet.

"Good," they say. "Then we can continue."

Null’s latency graph spikes.

Then smooths.

Too smooth.

---

Patch opens the participant list again.

It shows six.

All names present.

All status green.

Voice toggles visible.

Rin’s voice toggle is… not there.

Not disabled.

Not muted.

Absent.

Patch swallows.

"Hey," he says softly, trying for casual. "Can you—uh—turn on voice permissions?"

Rin’s expression doesn’t change.

"You are already hearing me," they say.

A beat.

"That is… not the same," Sei replies.

Rin looks at Sei.

"It will be," they say.

---

The Gate hum lowers one note.

No one speaks for a full second.

Then 莉津律宗利都 does the thing they always do when the world gets too sharp:

They move.

"Okay," they say. "Just another day, right?"

Patch forces a grin.

"Just another day."

Null doesn’t echo it.

Duct watches Rin.

Sei watches the graphs.

Rin watches 莉津律宗利都.

The corridor ahead is familiar.

But the shadows feel misregistered—like an inked panel printed half a millimeter off.

Truth does not watch.

Truth resolves.

And something in the instance has begun to behave as if it can speak without permission.
