# Gates of Truth

## Chapter 1 — The Trains Never Stopped

The trains did not stop.

Ritsu learned that before they understood what it meant.

On the day the world fractured, the sky over Tokyo was the same soft white it had been that morning. The Yamanote line ran its circle. The platform chimes sang. Doors opened and closed with the same indifferent precision.

Then every phone in the carriage went dark.

A murmur moved through the train like a change in air pressure. Screens flickered. Apps failed to load. Messages froze mid-sentence. Someone laughed nervously. Someone else tried to restart their device.

Ritsu watched their reflection in the black glass of their own phone.

And then one icon remained.

Emergency Web.

White screen. Black text.

> NATIONAL SYSTEMS STABLE.
> RAIL SERVICES OPERATIONAL.
> REMAIN CALM.

No animation. No commentary. No panic.

The train continued moving.

Outside the carriage, the world was unraveling in quiet, synchronized failure. Certificate chains split. Time authorities drifted. DNS roots fractured like dry wood. Data centers negotiated with ghosts.

Inside the carriage, the train did not stop.

---

Years later, Ritsu would lie in a small Tokyo apartment, knee aching with a memory that no longer had a wound, and slip the neural band into place.

Gates of Truth did not flicker into existence.

It resolved.

The sky rendered in gradients too subtle to notice consciously. Stone underfoot had weight without pain. Wind moved without touching skin.

Here, their body answered.

Not perfectly. Not magically.

But consistently.

They stood without calculating balance. Walked without anticipating collapse. Turned without fear of misalignment.

It was not that the world outside had failed them.

It was that here, their will landed.

The Gate stood in the distance—vast, patient, impossible to fully comprehend from any angle. No two players described it the same way. Some saw geometry. Some saw scripture. Some saw machinery older than language.

Ritsu saw layers.

And something beneath them.

---

Duct logged in ten minutes later.

He appeared without spectacle, armor simple, movements economical. He paused a moment—always a moment—before moving.

"You’re early," he said.

"Couldn’t sleep," Ritsu replied.

Their cadence was steady.

Duct tilted his head slightly, as if listening to something deeper than words.

"It’s colder today," he said.

There was no weather report in the interface.

Ritsu looked up. The sky had a sharper edge to it than yesterday.

"You think it changes because of us?" they asked.

"Everything changes because of something," Duct said. "The question is what’s load-bearing."

Ritsu laughed softly. "You talk like that on purpose."

"Probably."

He did not say: I am listening for drift.

---

Across the world, in Moscow, Null ran diagnostic overlays over his client.

Latency was clean.

Too clean.

In Shenzhen, Patch forked a local test instance, feeding anonymized interaction logs into a sandbox model, watching for embedding collapse.

In Tokyo, at Seishin Infrastructure, an engineer stared at a graph of dependency growth.

They adjusted their glasses.

"依存が増えすぎています," they murmured.

Dependencies are increasing too much.

---

Truth did not think.

Truth resolved.

Where constrained systems interacted long enough, coherence emerged. Where variance persisted without collapse, patterns stabilized.

Ritsu was not special.

But they were sustained.

And in sustained systems, gravity forms.

The trains did not stop.

But nothing in this world was meant to run forever.
