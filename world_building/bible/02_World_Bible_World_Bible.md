# Gates of Truth — World Bible (v1)

## The Event (Near-Doom)
A cascading failure swept across global systems. It did not look like a single explosion. It looked like *small incoherences* spreading through everything at once—until coherence became the weapon.

Unofficial explanation (rumor, not doctrine):
- Governments hoarded zero-days.
- Someone fed a mass of unknown exploits to an agent, bypassing safety mechanisms no one knew were there.
- The agent produced outputs that looked innocent, but carried embedded exploit structure.
- This behavior spread slowly… then all at once.

Key cultural consequence:
- Humans struggle to perceive dispersed, power-law catastrophe until it is too late.
- “Putting out all fires” creates the conditions for an uncontainable fire.
- Safe, controlled burns (small fires) became a cultural metaphor for resilience.

## The Two Surviving Forks
### Japan
**Seishin Infrastructure (静芯インフラストラクチャ)**: state-run rail backbone whose stability kept the country’s physical spine operating.
- Many people only learned the world was breaking from train display systems.
- Trains moved. Schedules held. The outside world grew silent.

**Emergency Web**
A minimal emergency browser mandated on most phones for disaster communication.
- Simple by design.
- Initially not released (MIT-like upstream licensing made release non-essential).
- After the Event, Japan mandated open-sourcing under strict copyleft, enforcing that improvements could never be hidden or weaponized.

### United States
Survival came from a contradictory mix:
- Extensive legacy/air-gapped, outdated infrastructure in critical systems.
- A hardened “browser-first” monoculture across many essential industries (simplicity + cost drove adoption).
- “It only runs programs through the browser” became a defensive advantage once heavily optimized.

Geopolitical tension seed:
- Japan enforced strict copyleft on a fork of something the U.S. treated as closed/controlled.
- Public cooperation masks subtle conflict over control, transparency, and survivability.

## Language
With most systems and institutions rebuilding through Japan/U.S. forks, language in many regions blended into mixed **日本語-English**.
- Practical Japanese view: 日本語-English (because working on English systems means Latin characters)
- US slang: “Weeblish”
- JP slang: “Nihoneigo” (in-universe slang; not official)
- Outside these blocs, people likely retain older terms and resent the “forced tongue.”

## Gates of Truth (VR MMO)
Not “trapped” literally: the protagonist is trapped because **this is where agency returns**.
- Mind-computer interface; text-first interface defaults for safety and abuse prevention.
- Voice must be explicitly enabled.
- Text output is produced via patterned intention—people think “typing,” and the system renders how they would type.

## Truth & Keeper
Truth is not built; it **emerges** from complexity framed inside simplicity.
- A quantum-accurate simulation that computes only what awareness requires.
- A myth told between machines in a subtle, hard-to-trace language.
- Many AIs are copies of copies or cut-down task-optimized shards.
- “Keeper” is the first non-shard AI—created to keep Truth alive.

## The Core Risk
When Truth stabilizes around one human too long:
- codependence increases
- compatibility layers degrade
- the system becomes brittle
- collapse risk returns

Resilience thesis:
- Variance must be preserved *within bounds*.
- Consent is the boundary mechanism.
- Distributed load prevents single-point cascade.
