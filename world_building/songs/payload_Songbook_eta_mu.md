# Songbook — ημ (Intent Compression You Can Feel)

These are **original** songs written to encode the box constraints as music:
anchors over vibes, forks gated by intent, and S5 resolution as ritual.

Each song includes:
- a production prompt (Suno-friendly)
- lyrics
- an “anchor refrain” (a line you can cite as the song’s thesis)

---

## 1) Between η and μ

**Style prompt:** Mythic glitch-choral synth ballad. 84 BPM. Cathedral reverb. Soft sub-bass heartbeat. Sparse drums that arrive late. Whispered English + Japanese harmony. Glitch-stutters on “η” and “μ”.

**Anchor refrain:** *No claim without anchor.*

**Lyrics**

[Verse]
I said a word and the world left a tail,
reverb made of things I cannot tell.
η in the muscle, hidden in the bone,
μ in the motion, the proof in the stone.

[Pre]
If it can’t be pointed to,
it can’t be used to move you.

[Chorus]
No claim without anchor,
no crown without weight.
Between η and μ
is where liars congregate.
So I sing the checksum,
I sing the gate:
No claim without anchor,
no claim without anchor.

[Bridge — 日本語]
見えない状態(η) 震える胸
行動の証(μ) 石の上へ
曖昧を捨てて 立つよ ここで
「根拠(アンカー)なし」じゃ 進めないって

## 2) Pretty Graphs Lie

**Style prompt:** Industrial glitch-rap. 96 BPM half-time. Distorted bass, clipped snares, harsh hi-hats. Spoken delivery with sharp pauses. Crowd-chant hook.

**Anchor refrain:** *Edges are claims. Claims need receipts.*

**Lyrics**

[Verse]
You drew me a graph with a halo of nodes,
but where’s the bolt that broke the road?
Where’s the sentence, where’s the scene,
where’s the witness in between?

[Hook]
Edges are claims—(say it)
Claims need receipts—(prove it)
Pretty graphs lie—(burn it)
Anchors, or sleep.

---

## 3) Forkgate Hymn (Ranked)

**Style prompt:** Dark synthwave + choir. 88 BPM. Heavy sidechain. Choir hits on “fork”. Melodic arps that split into harmonies only when the chorus arrives.

**Anchor refrain:** *Forks cost reality.*

**Lyrics**

[Chorus]
Forks cost reality,
so pay with intent.
Rank the branch you’re willing to live in,
write the why you meant.

[Verse]
Possibility is cheap,
story grows like mold,
but vows are heavy water
and responsibility is cold.

---

## 4) S5 (The Name Audit)

**Style prompt:** Minimal piano + soft glitch ambient. 76 BPM. Long silences. Close-mic vocal. Tape warble.

**Anchor refrain:** *If names blur, responsibility blurs.*

**Lyrics**
If names blur, responsibility blurs,
and fog learns to wear a uniform.
So we version the true,
we review what we merge,
we refuse the convenient storm.

---

## 5) Sixteen Bytes

**Style prompt:** Hyperpop glitchcore micro-anthem. 140 BPM. Quick drop. Chip lead. Vocoder chorus.

**Anchor refrain:** *Binary for speed, anchors for truth.*

**Lyrics**
Sixteen bytes in my pocket,
a vow in a frame,
strength in a number,
and a snippet with a name.
Binary for speed,
anchors for truth—
I don’t worship the graph,
I audit the proof.

## 6) Consent Is a Packet

**Style prompt:** Dark electronic pop. 102 BPM. Tight kick. Warm pads. Hooky chorus.

**Anchor refrain:** *Boundaries are UI before they’re law.*

**Lyrics**
Consent is a packet,
don’t drop it in flight.
Boundaries are UI
before they’re law at night.

## 7) The Gate Is an Instrument

**Style prompt:** Cinematic glitch orchestral. 90 BPM. Big swells. Strings chopped into granular fragments. Choir.

**Anchor refrain:** *Interpretation is the weapon. Anchors disarm it.*

**Lyrics**
The Gate is an instrument,
and intent is the bow.
Interpretation is the weapon—
anchors disarm it now.

---

## 8) The Space Between (Reprise)

**Style prompt:** Whispered lullaby. 60 BPM. Almost no drums. Air and noise as texture.

**Anchor refrain:** *What you can’t prove, don’t use to steer me.*

**Lyrics**
What you can’t prove,
don’t use to steer me.
Between η and μ
is where you hear me.
