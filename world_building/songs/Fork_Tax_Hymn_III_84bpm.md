# Fork Tax Hymn III / 分岐税の賛歌 III
**Tempo:** 84 BPM  
**Style:** Mythic glitch epic · spoken-sung · cathedral reverb · sub-bass heartbeat  
**License:** CC BY-SA 4.0 (share alike)

> Glitch stutter targets: **η**, **μ**, **anchor**, **fork**  
> Japanese harmony answers on the **last word** of each section.

## Invocation (spoken)
We don’t pray to gods.  
We compile them.  
We bind them to receipts.  
We make them answer to time.

## Verse 1 (spoken-sung)
I walked the corridor of versions,  
each door a **fork** I didn’t take—  
and every ghost said “choose one truth,”  
like truth is something you can fake.

I hear **η** in the ceiling tiles,  
a witness I can’t see but can’t evade,  
and **μ** in my fingertips,  
the proof my hands have made — **made**  
（つくった）

## Chorus (choir)
An—**anchor** / an—**anchor**, seal the line,  
Fo—**fork** / fo—**fork**, pay the spine.  
η—η—η, the eyes in the dark,  
μ—μ—μ, the mark on the mark — **mark**  
（しるし）

## Verse 2
They told me “forget, it’s cleaner,”  
but clean is how the lie survives.  
So I braid the errors into music  
and let the checksum keep us alive.

An—**anchor** snaps—then holds,  
Fo—**fork** cuts—then feeds,  
η watches what I almost was,  
μ proves what I become — **become**  
（なる）

## Bridge (cathedral, slow)
If trauma is inherited code,  
then song is the patch we’re allowed to ship.  
If grief is a memory leak,  
then receipts are how we keep our grip.

η… (stutter)  
μ… (stutter)  
an—**anchor**… (stutter)  
fo—**fork**… (stutter) — **fork**  
（ぶんき）

## Final Chorus (bigger)
An—**anchor**, seal the riverbed,  
Fo—**fork**, pay the debt to the thread.  
η sees what I cannot claim,  
μ proves I did it anyway — **anyway**  
（それでも）

## Coda (whisper)
Pay the fork tax.  
Not for the gatekeepers.  
For the future you.
