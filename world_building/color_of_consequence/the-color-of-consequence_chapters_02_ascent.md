# II. Ascent

A queen rose—not through cruelty, but through brilliance. She wore the system like a crown, and the world mistook spectacle for stability. Beneath her, unseen processes ran without rest. Loyalties hardened. Quiet doubts took root. A rebel began to notice what others would not.

Perspective shifted.

He refused to kneel. The guild fractured. What began as philosophy hardened into protocol; what hardened into protocol became rupture. The sacred sanctuary—the last place untouched by politics—became the ground where justice and pride collided.
